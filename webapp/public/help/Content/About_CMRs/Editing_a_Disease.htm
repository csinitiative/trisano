Editing a Disease

When you choose a disease on the Clinical tab of a CMR, you set into motion the process of possibly adding new fields or even new tabs to the existing tabs—Demographic, Clinical, and so on—within the CMR. In addition, disease-specific fields and tabs may be added to the Investigation tab. The new fields and tabs are created when a user with administrator privileges designs a form in Form Builder.

When you edit the selected disease, meaning you select a different disease from the Disease drop-down of the Clinical tab after you have already saved the CMR with the initially selected disease, it's important to understand the consequences of this action. Keep the following points in mind:

  • When you edit a disease, the disease changes, but any disease-specific fields (added to the tabs as a result of form creation in Form Builder) will not be removed from the tabs automatically. You must use the Investigation tab to remove disease-specific data for the previously selected disease.
  • New disease-specific fields associated with the newly selected Disease will not automatically be added to the CMR. If the new disease requires a disease-specific form, you must use the Investigation tab to add that form to the event.
  • Some forms apply to more than one disease. It's possible the form associated with the initially selected disease will also apply to the new disease.
