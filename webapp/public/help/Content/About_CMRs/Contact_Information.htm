Contact Information

A contact is a person who the patient may have been in close proximity with, may have caught something from or may have exposed to an illness. Collecting contact information allows health officials to track as many forms of exposure and contact as possible. Enter as much information as you know. The Last name field is required.

Note: Depending on the disease you select, different questions appear on the CMR tabs. These questions are specific to the disease and appear once you have made the choice and selected one of the Save options. For more information about diseases, read Choosing a Disease and Editing a Disease.

 

Field Description
Last name Enter the last name of the contact or search for an existing contact.
First name Enter the first name of the contact.
Disposition Select a disposition from the list.
Contact type Select a contact type from the list.
Phone type Select the type of the phone number supplied by the contact.
Area code Enter the area code for the contact's phone number.
Phone number Enter the phone number for the contact. You can enter the number with or without the dash.
Extension Enter the extension, if any, to select when calling the contact.

 

After you have entered the information for the contact fields listed in the above table, click Save & Continue. When the contact information is saved, it creates a new contact event for the original patient.

To add more information about the contact, click Edit Contact. Additional entry fields are displayed in the lower part of the page that are the same as those on the Demographic tab for a CMR.

In the Address area of the Demographic tab is a button called Copy From Original Patient. Click this button to import the address information from the original patient if the information for this section is the same.

In addition, the following tabbed pages are added. Explanations for these fields are the same as for the tabbed pages for the original patient:

  • Clinical
  • Laboratory
  • Epidemiological
  • Investigation
  • Notes

The fields on these pages are the same as those for the original patient. Click the links in the bulleted list for explanations of the fields.

Mapping Contacts

After you have entered and saved contacts for a patient, you may see a link at the bottom of the Contacts page called Show Contacts Map. This feature allows you to view the addresses of the patient's contacts displayed on a map. Refer to Mapping Contacts for more information.
