Mapping Place Exposures

The mapping feature allows you to display a map that contains tags showing the location of the patient and any of the Place Exposures recorded on the Epidemiological tab associated with a CMR for the patient. This allows you to see in a graphical way, the proximity of the places the patient has been exposed to.

 

To map places:

  1. Click Events in the upper right corner of the page.
  2. Locate the patient whose place exposures you want to map and click either Show or Edit. You do not have to be in Edit Mode to map the places for the patient.
  3. Select the Epidemiological tab. You'll see the place exposures for the patient listed as in the following illustration.

 

 

  1. Click Show Map of Places. A map of the patient and the listed places opens just below the place list as shown in the following illustration. The red tag indicates the address of the patient and the blue tags indicate the addresses of the places.

 

 

  1. Move the mouse over the blue tags on the map to see a small tool tip that names the place exposure for each tag.

 

 

Note: If any of the places are missing map coordinates, you'll see a message next to the Show Map of Places link indicating this as shown in the following illustration. When you see this message, you will have to check each of the places and update its coordinates before that place will appear on the map.
