Overview of Utah NEDSS Project

 

The Utah Department of Health (UDOH), the Department of Technology Services (DTS) and local health department (LHD) officials are working with the Collaborative Software Initiative to collaborate in the development of a disease reporting and surveillance system for the state of Utah that is NEDSS compatible and compliant with the Public Information Network. This project is funded by a grant from Novell to CSI for the cost of development and provide a bespoke, open source, project to meet the needs of the State of Utah and, once released, to be extensible and configurable to meet the needs of other jurisdictions as well.

Major components:

  • infectious disease surveillance subsystem
  • case management subsystem
  • outbreak management subsystem
  • database designed to support both patient-centric and case/outbreak-centric views of the recorded data
  • report generation to CDC NEDSS and to UDOH
  • electronic transmission of information (migrate from paper, fax, and phone) to and from local health departments (LHDs) UDOH and the CDC

 

Components need to have these high-level capabilities:

  • scalable, extensible design supporting both high-availability and disaster recovery
  • integral security capability to protect confidential patient information web UI for access from by any authorized, web connected user with access to a web browser
  • analytic (business intelligence) access to the data for ad hoc analysis and reporting
  • local (laptop oriented) application with server synchronization (asynchronous updates) for occasionally connected field users
  • wireless (PDA, laptop, perhaps phone) access to critical functions for key executive and field users
