A project contains all the source code, emails, announcements, and other elements that people work on together to produce a result. You can define a project, select tools for your project members to use, and create pages for information you want to share. You can use CollabNet project pages to coordinate the efforts of many developers in different locations.

Here are some initial steps to get you started.

Step 1: Customize your project pages.

You can structure your project information the way you want it by adding and editing pages, subpages and freeform pages.

Step 2: Customize your artifacts.

An artifact represents a unit of work that moves through your development process until completion.

Step 3: Add people to the project.

You can bring in other people to work on your project by inviting them or adding them to join the member list. You must assign each member a role in the project.

Step 4: Communicate with project members.

You can stay in touch with other people on the project by joining mailing lists, participating in discussions, and following project announcements.

Step 5: Share project documents.

You can store and share project documents, such as specifications, project plans, requirement lists, or test plans.
